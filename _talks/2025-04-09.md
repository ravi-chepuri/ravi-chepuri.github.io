Oral presentation about heterogeneity in reservoir computing.

**Abstract:** Reservoir computers (RCs) are a type of neural network that excel at forecasting chaotic dynamical systems. However, principles for tuning their hyperparameters remain unclear, particularly
regarding the impact of heterogeneity in individual neuron properties, such as degree (number of
connections). A simple one-dimensional mean-field approximation to RC dynamics offers a limited but useful description of average node activation and oscillatory onset, though it applies only
to homogeneous-degree RCs. Here, we refine this approximation to explicitly account for degree
heterogeneity, enabling more accurate predictions for Erdós–Rényi-like and power-law degree distributions. Our analysis shows that heterogeneity shifts the region of hyperparameter space where
non-saturated neuron activation occurs, as well as regions of good task performance,
guiding further investigation into hyperparameter tuning in heterogeneous reservoirs.
